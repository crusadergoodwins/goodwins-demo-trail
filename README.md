# Stronghold Crusader Extreme DEMO Campaign trail mod by Goodwins.

### How to play:
1) Download the whole mod.
2) Make a backup copy of your original game folder.
3) Pick a version in your desirable language (available only in English or Russian).
4) Copy the insides of the "bundle" folder all together into the game folder.
5) Open the game using "Goodwins DEMO trail.exe"
6) Have fun!

### Important notes:
• The mod will only run in Stronghold Crusader EXTREME (v1.41) (Magic is disabled by me)

• The campaign itself should always be played on the original and unedited "Goodwins DEMO trail.exe" file as downloaded. Editing it or using UCP updater on it may overwrite and disable the ideal settings for the trail.

• If you still wish to make a change through the UCP patcher (for example change starting gold or player colour) - make a copy of the .exe and use the copy for your custom skirmish games. Alternatively, contact the author for help.

### The DEMO trail bundle includes:
- 2 new AI lords with their icons and messages (text, audio and video). Only relevant enemy messages are included at this stage. Allied messages will be released along the actual trail.
- Game text file which now includes the new lords names, messages and descriptions, as well as campaign mission names.
- 3 custom maps for the 3 missions on the DEMO trail.
- The DEMO trail itself.
- .exe file that includes all the updated AIC files, starting resources, troops and customized game settings and mechanics. Use it to start the game.
- A couple small surprises to improve immersion.

# About my project:
The project includes:
## 1) 3 new AI lords:
  
  ***Mira DeMalier*** replaces the Marshal
  
  Mira is an early-game menace that falls off in the mid-game, but comes back with a vengeance in later phases. She is good at everything she does... but great at nothing. Mira is an agent of chaos on the map. She is everywhere and always. As a simple rule - if there is a Mira on the map - the whole game will center around her. Her spirit animal is a shark - if she ever tastes blood, she will never let go and there is no coming back.
  
  ***Viggo, "The Iron Wolf"*** replaces the Sultan.
  
  Viggo is an absolute titan of defensive gameplay. He is almost impossible to break, and sieging him is always a challenge. Other than that, Viggo's early game attacks may cause a smile or even laughter. However, Iron Wolf ramps up really fast in the mid-game and quickly becomes a serious offensive threat. His attacks are fast and vicious, often resembling a machine gun. Viggo is a great siege-buddy, as his attacking tactic mostly revolves around clearing the defenders off their walls and sometimes burning the castle. He is not as good though at actually killing his target. Other than his relentless mid-game attacks, Viggo's other biggest strength is his close range combat. Having this guy as your neighbour can sometimes really be a nightmare.
  
  ***The General*** replaces Nizar

  The strongest lord in this version of the game, General Robert Quain is this campaigns' final boss. He does not show up in the DEMO version, only debuting on the "United Front" mission during the actual campaign. The General is by far the best attacker in the game, and his sieges are akin to a hurricane or a tsunami. While others dance around your porch, the General knocks down your front door and walks in with his shoes on. Other than being a deadly force of nature, General Quain is great at making money for the war buisiness. His castles resemble huge military bases and are filled with countless traps and unpleasant surprises for those who dare set foot inside.
  
##  2) Each of the other 13 original lords recieves improved attacking and defensive capabilities, better economy and improved castles. 
### For each lord I make sure to stay as close to the original spirit as possible, keeping the same oh so familiar identity, but just slightly modernised and improved to suit modern day (better) players. 
### Castles (aiv) are mixed. There are 4 types of them:
#### • made by me from scratch (based on vanilla style)
#### • taken from EvreyImproved aiv pack and improved further by me (big thanks to Evrey for some ideas and inspiration)
#### • taken from the well known and loved vanilla castles and improved by me
#### • there are 2 or 3 castles that are taken from Tatha and improved by me
#### In general for each castle I tried to keep it as close to the original as possible, while improving economy, defensive capabilities and fire resistance, with an extra focus on taking the least amount of space and sometimes also on the early game build path (on low money)
### An example of an improved vanilla Lord (Snake):
####  The Snake is known for being overly aggressive, especially early on, and swarming the map with countless archers while attempting to burn your economy down and scale your walls. His castles are a mixture of stone and water, and his economy is mostly just woodwork. My version of the Snake has all that, but slightly better. He now uses a single Fire Ballista when attacking, which helps with both ranged combat and the whole "burn them down" thing. He now uses a Siege Tower in his attacks which helps him scale your walls with more than just ladders, and some Shields to cover for his archers. Additionally, the Snake got his original weakness of dealing no actual damage fixed (OG Snake attacks lacked proper melee units, only using a bunch of weak spearmen, resulting in him often not being able to kill even a defenseless enemy lord). Now the Snake uses several assassins in his attacks to help him scale your walls (again) and bring higher damage. He also digs your moat with some macemen, which do a better job at that and also bring more damage. As a trade-off for using these more expensive units, the Snake now builds x1 Chapel in every castle to help with taxes, plus he stores more wood on the stockpile and has better bow/spear production which allows him to freely sell extra weapons for money. Duc Beauregard now has better gatehouses, two adjacent layers of low wall/high wall/moat with x1 Tower Ballista against lone catapults, and better defensive archers and slingers.

  ## 3) Unique, fun, memorable and challenging **CAMPAIGN TRAIL/TRAILS** that use the updated lords and their castles. 
  ### The campaign trail is a core part of the project and is the sole reason for why I even started doing all this. Each mission is carefully crafted to provide the player with a unique experience, while challenging the familiar norms and old habbits, forcing the player to use and hone their wit, problem-solving skills and creativity in order to find a seemingly impossible solution and overcome any hardships along the way.
  #### Every mission either tests or teaches you a specific set of skills, revolves around different mechanics and requires different approach. You are advised to play them IN ORDER. Playing the trail out of order is HARDER THAN IT SHOULD BE. For example, let's say that Mission #5 teaches you to do A, while mission #9 tests A and teaches you B. If you skip them and jump directly to mission #11 which tests both A and B and teaches you C, you will have an extra hard time dealing with it.
  
### ***Mission types and RUSHING***:  
I have spent countless days on each of the missions, testing them, updating and perfecting them to follow the needed scenario while maintaining the desired difficulty. Some missions are harder than others, some are easier (stress relief is very important). There are Outpost missions, missions with no Market, missions with open castles (no walls/moat), and various other kinds of missions. I tried my best to make each mission immune to rush, and while most of them are indeed rush-proof, some may still be rusheable. I ask you, the player, to NOT abuse that fact and to NOT rush the enemy. Rushing is only acceptable on #14 "Swan Song", where you will be given a choice to either recruit 30 crossbowmen and play properly vs all the enemies, or give up on crossbowmen and rush one of the enemies with 30 macemen instead.
